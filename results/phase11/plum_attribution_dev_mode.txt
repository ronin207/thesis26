Observations
------------
1. **The headline thesis claim materialises.** Inside the zkVM, the
   Griffin-hasher PLUM verifier is 54.07× more expensive than the
   SHA3-hasher variant. The Merkle-tree shape is identical (both
   hashers do 1,598 compressions); the entire 54× gap is attributable
   to the per-compression cost of Griffin vs SHA3.

2. **Fp192 muls dominate inside Griffin.** The Griffin variant runs
   8.4 million MORE Fp192 muls than the SHA3 variant; almost all of
   those extra muls live inside the 1,853 Griffin permutations. Per-
   permutation: ~4,530 Fp192 muls × ~2,295 cyc/mul ≈ 10.4M cycles,
   matching the 10.5M cyc/Griffin number directly.

3. **The Fp192 mul cost is the load-bearing primitive.** ~2,300–3,200
   cycles per multiplication. That's the variable-time BigUint
   backend cost in zkVM cycles. Phase 1.5 of the implementation plan
   (swap to constant-time 4×u64 + Barrett/Montgomery) would reduce
   this dramatically; without that swap, the absolute numbers are
   the BigUint-on-rv32 cost, not a fundamental property of Fp192.

4. **PRF evals are individually large.** Each PRF eval is ~700M cycles
   in the Griffin variant — that's the 199-bit modular exponentiation
   `(K + I_ℓ)^((p-1)/t)`. There are only 28 of these in verify (= B),
   so they account for ~20B / 19.5B ≈ 100% of Griffin verify cycles
   if attributed at face value — but actually most of those 700M
   cycles ARE the Fp192 muls inside the modular exp, so this is
   double-counting. The "true" attribution would need to deduplicate.

5. **Hash compressions same count, wildly different cost.** Both
   variants do exactly 1,598 hash compresses (Merkle paths through
   the same depths for the same query counts). SHA3 = 226K cyc each,
   Griffin = 12.2M cyc each. The 54× ratio matches the total cycle
   ratio exactly — confirming that the hasher choice IS the
   dominant cost driver.

6. **Sign-side cost is much higher** (as expected). Griffin sign
   takes 17.5s of host CPU vs 0.22s for SHA3 — a 80× gap. Most of
   that goes into the 43,008 Griffin permutations during signing
   (vs 1,853 during verify), reflecting that sign builds the full
   tree while verify only traverses paths.

Limitations
-----------
- Dev-proof mode: the prover is mocked. Real prove mode would give
  the same cycle counts but produce a valid receipt; expect minutes
  to hours per variant on consumer hardware.
- BigUint Fp192 backend: variable-time, ~3K cycles per mul. Real
  measurement should swap to the Phase 1.5 constant-time backend.
- t_M for final-poly check = pp.kappas[R] = 13. This is implementer
  heuristic, not paper-attested. Affects the cycle counts only at
  the 5%-ish level (the t_M × η = 52 extra fiber openings vs total
  verifier work).
- Setup deviation: our U_i descents are η rather than the paper's
  η², requiring the forbidden-residue filter on final-poly bases.
  Cycle impact is negligible (~30 extra cycles per FS oversample).
