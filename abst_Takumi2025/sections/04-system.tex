\section{System Design}
We construct a custom \emph{precompile suite} that bypasses field emulation
for the primitives that dominate the cost, mirroring how zkVMs already ship
precompiles for standardized primitives (e.g.\ SHA-2, secp256k1). Three AIR
modules are specified, in expected cycle-share order: (i) the Griffin
permutation~\cite{cryptoeprint:2022/403} over PLUM's $192$-bit prime field, the load-bearing $91\%$
target; (ii) multi-limb $192$-bit modular arithmetic over $p = 2^{64}p_0+1$;
and (iii) the $t$-th power-residue PRF symbol check ($t=256$ fixed), a
constant-exponent routine for $a^{(p-1)/t} \bmod p$. Each module ships with a
written soundness argument tying its AIR constraints to the reference
specification, so the precompile layer preserves the scheme's unforgeability
under the assumption that the zkVM's cross-table lookup argument is sound.
